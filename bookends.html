Morning Ignition

Prime your neurology, name your #1 revenue move, and build an activation runway so deep work starts without friction.

0 / 8
Phase 1 — Wake the Body
No phone for 10 minutes
Leave it plugged in across the room. Checking messages first thing floods your brain with other people's priorities and spikes reactive cortisol. You need proactive cortisol.
Anti-Dopamine Drain
Sunlight + water + 20 reps of anything
Step outside or stand at a bright window for 2–5 min. Drink a full glass of water. Do bodyweight squats, push-ups, or jumping jacks — just enough to signal "we're awake" to your nervous system.
5 minCortisol + Dopamine Sync
Phase 2 — Prime the Mind
Identity statement + daily intention
Write one sentence: "I am someone who ___." Then your intention for the day — not a to-do, but a way of being. ("Today I operate with calm urgency." / "Today I ship, not perfect.")
3 minPrefrontal Activation
Gratitude anchor — one specific thing
From yesterday, with a sensory detail. ("The quiet in my apartment at 7am with coffee by the window.") Specificity rewires your brain; generic gratitudes don't shift state.
1 minNervous System Regulation
5-minute meditation or breathwork
Box breathing (4-4-4-4) or a guided session. Non-negotiable for ADHD — the only daily practice proven to physically thicken your prefrontal cortex. Even 5 min counts.
5 minExecutive Function Primer
Phase 3 — Aim the Business
Name your #1 Revenue-Moving Action
"If I could only do ONE thing today that moves me closer to revenue, what is it?" Pre-revenue means every day includes at least one action from: build product, talk to users, or sell.
3 minRevenue-Critical
Set your Top 3 tasks (max)
Your #1 plus two supporting tasks. Three feels achievable; ten feels paralyzing. Everything else lives on a backlog you don't look at until these are done.
3 minAnti-Overwhelm
Pre-load your first deep work sprint
Open the file/app/doc you'll start with. Set the visual timer. Queue the focus playlist. Remove every decision between "routine done" and "working" — the transition gap is where ADHD brains lose the whole morning.
2 minActivation Runway
ADHD Founder Tip

Do this entire protocol before you open email, Slack, or social media. The second you check messages, your brain switches from commander mode to responder mode — and getting it back costs 20+ minutes of executive function you can't afford pre-revenue.

≈ 20 minutes · 1–2 hours before sleep

Evening Shutdown

Close open loops. Capture wins. Load tomorrow's runway so morning-you can launch without deciding anything.

0 / 7
Phase 1 — Close the Day
Revenue action review
Did you complete your #1 revenue action? Yes → write what happened and what you learned. No → no shame, write why and what blocked you. Every day teaches you something if you capture it.
3 minFounder Intelligence
Win logging — find three
Write 3 wins from today, any size. "Sent the scary email." "Worked 2 focused hours." "Didn't spiral after the rejection." Pre-revenue is emotionally brutal — retrain your brain to register progress.
3 minAnti-Negativity Bias
Brain dump — clear every open loop
Write down every lingering thought, task, worry, or idea still bouncing around your head. Don't organize, just dump. This externalizes the "unfinished task anxiety" that keeps ADHD brains up at 2am.
3 minOpen Loop Closure
Phase 2 — Load Tomorrow
Pre-decide tomorrow's #1 revenue action
Look at your brain dump + business priorities and choose tomorrow's most important task. Write it on a sticky note or at the top of your journal. Morning-you doesn't decide — evening-you already did.
2 minDecision Pre-Load
Set up the physical environment
Lay out workout clothes. Fill a water bottle. Clear your desk. Open the doc/app you'll start with tomorrow. Charge your phone outside the bedroom. Each removes one micro-decision that could derail your morning.
3 minFriction Elimination
Phase 3 — Signal Shutdown
Say "Shutdown complete" out loud
Sounds silly. Works. A verbal phrase tells your brain the workday is officially over. If a work thought intrudes later, respond internally: "That's been captured. I'll handle it tomorrow."
5 secCognitive Boundary
Transition to low-stimulation mode
Dim lights. Phone on DND or in another room. No business content, no "quick" email checks. Read fiction, stretch, or talk to someone you love. Your nervous system needs a clear signal that survival mode is off.
Nervous System Down-Reg
Pre-Revenue Reality Check

The temptation to "just do one more thing" at night is strongest when you're pre-revenue because everything feels urgent. But exhausted founders make bad decisions, write bad copy, and burn out before they ship. Rest is a revenue strategy. Protect it like one.
